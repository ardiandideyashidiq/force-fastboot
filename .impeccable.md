## Design Context

### Users
Android enthusiasts flashing custom ROMs and firmware on their own devices at home. They sit at a desk, often at night, with the phone connected via USB. They're technical but not necessarily professional — they value clear feedback, safety cues, and a tool that feels as refined as the devices they work on.

### Brand Personality
**Minimalist · Surgical · Elegant**

The tool should feel like a precision instrument — every click deliberate, every pixel purposeful. Low ego, high confidence. The "paw" in pawflash suggests a subtle animal motif (soft, warm counterpoint to cold hardware), but executed with restraint.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Premium polished** — CleanMyMac / Tower / Sublime Text lineage. Spacious layouts, refined typography, subtle glass-and-depth effects, soft shadows. Dark mode native (used in low-light settings). Not loud or aggressive; the drama comes from precision, not from neon accents or glowing gradients.

- **Theme**: Dark (existing), with warm-tinted neutrals (OKLCH hue ~50-55) to counteract cold blue blacks
- **Accent**: Existing purple (#aa3bff / oklch ~0.58 0.22 300) — keep it as the single accent, use sparingly
- **Anti-reference**: Avoid cyberpunk/hacker neon, avoid generic AI dark dashboards, avoid over-charting

### Design Principles
1. **Surgical precision** — every element has a clear job. No decorative cruft, no redundant labels, no sparklines
2. **Calm confidence** — the tool erases bricks. The UI should feel reassuringly stable, never frantic or "techy"
3. **Subtle depth** — light glass effects, soft elevation, spatial hierarchy without heavy borders or cards-inside-cards
4. **Typography as voice** — clear hierarchy, generous measure, refined type. The type system *is* the decoration
5. **Restrained play** — small moments of warmth (paw motif, micro-animations, transition ease) without being cute
6. **Information density on demand** — start clean, reveal complexity through progressive disclosure
